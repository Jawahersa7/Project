Generating Knowledge and Awareness

At our company, we talked to people about the problems blind individuals face in public places.

We used a questionnaire to gather their thoughts.

Most said it’s hard for blind people to move around because there aren’t enough tools to help them, and they often feel unsafe.

They also want something that gives them directions in real-time and helps them be independent without needing help all the time.

Many also mentioned that public places should be better designed for blind people. These ideas helped us create a product that solves these problems.

Approach to Innovation

Product Description: Our product is called VisionDirection, a pair of smart glasses designed to help people who are visually impaired. These glasses have a camera that looks around and sends information to the user through headphones. The glasses tell the user what’s in front of them, helping them avoid obstacles and walk safely. The goal is to make it easier for blind individuals to move around by themselves and feel confident.

Product Specifications:

Camera: A small camera on the glasses that helps "see" what's around.

Audio Feedback: Headphones that speak to the user, telling them what the camera sees.

AI Technology: Smart technology that understands what the camera sees and gives clear instructions to the user.

Voice Commands: Users can talk to the glasses to control them without using their hands.

Lightweight Design: The glasses are light and comfortable to wear all day.

Battery: The battery lasts up to 12 hours before it needs to be charged.

Bluetooth: The glasses can connect to a smartphone using Bluetooth for extra features.

Durable: The glasses are strong and can handle everyday wear and tear.
